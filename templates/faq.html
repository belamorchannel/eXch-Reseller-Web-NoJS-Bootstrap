What is aggregation?

We operate two BTC address pools: mixed and aggregated. Mixed pools obscure transaction origins for privacy but may raise risk scores. Aggregated pools use a single known address, reducing privacy but ensuring compatibility with major exchanges.

Aggregated Pool:

Pros:

  • • Low risk score, no freezes at major exchanges
  • • Accepted by Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, etc.

Cons:

  • • None

Mixed Pool:

Pros:

  • • Strong privacy
  • • Nearly untraceable
  • • P2P-like

Cons:

  • • High risk score, potential freezes at exchanges

Why is my Bitcoin payout taking so long?

If you chose slow fee mode, we used the lowest mining fee, leading to delays (12 hours to a week) based on Bitcoin’s mempool. Use CPFP in your wallet to speed it up, or select quick mode (with a fee) for faster confirmation.

Why is my Monero order delayed?

Monero’s protocol requires 10 confirmations for UTXOs to be spendable. Using a single wallet for all transactions may delay orders as we process them sequentially.

Why is my Bitcoin Lightning order in error?

Bitcoin Lightning is still evolving. Common issues include:

  • • Android battery management: Keep your wallet app (e.g., Phoenix) in the foreground or disable battery optimization.
  • • Inbound liquidity: Ensure your wallet can receive payments.

Contact us with your wallet software name for help.

Why no Telegram?

Telegram lacks end-to-end encryption for desktop apps and bots, making it insecure for sensitive data. We avoid mobile devices due to hardware backdoor risks. See here and discussion.

Warning: Avoid Telegram bots for exchanges; they store data unencrypted and lack privacy.
